About me

Stargazing into the clear Kansas skies throughout my childhood inspired my interest in astrophysics. One of my favorite memories growing up was creating an astronomy club at my high school and going on stargazing adventures with my friends. I lived in a suburb but only a short drive away from the city lights there would be vast open prairies with crystal clear night skies. Figure 1 shows a picture of the Kansas sunset taken by one of my friends before a night of stargazing.

I decided to to pursue an academic career in astrophysics starting with going to the University of Washington, Seattle from 2015-2019. By my first week of school, I was already doing research thanks to the Pre-Major Astronomy Program. I quickly realized that much of modern astrophysics research is essentially applied data science. For example, my projects would require a literature review (business understanding), advanced statistical techniques to summarize data (exploratory data analysis) and paper-quality data visualization using Python. I enjoyed leveraging these skills/tools to address exciting questions about the universe.

I enjoyed it so much that I decided to get my PhD in astrophysics at the University of California, Riverside (2019-2024). It was difficult at first.

Discuss how, on top of that, I went through cardiac arrest. It made me re-think my career trajectory. I actually became a farmer for a bit. Main conclusion is that I want to do work that has a real-world impact (like feeding people, sustainable practices, and community building). I decided to finish what I started (my PhD) but I would actively prepared myself for a transition into industry.
