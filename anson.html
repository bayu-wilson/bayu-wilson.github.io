Fluorescent Ionization Fronts at the tail-end of reionization

Recent models of reionization predict that large-scale opacity fluctuations in the z < 6 Lyman-alpha forest are generated by mpc-scale islands of neutral hydrogen. During the last phases of reionization, ionizing radiation from star-forming galaxies chisel down the last remaining neutral islands as ionization fronts (I-fronts) close in on all sides. The boundaries of these neutral islands possess the ideal conditions for producing Lyman-alpha line radiation. I conducted a parameter space study which aims to isolate the main dependencies of I-front fluorescence and explores the possibility of directly observing the line radiation. A detection of neutral islands at z < 6 would be smoking gun evidence for a late-ending reionization process. I am currently writing a paper that will be published in a peer-reviewed journal in 2023.
